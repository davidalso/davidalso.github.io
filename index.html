How to play: Use your arrow keys to move the atomic tiles. When two tiles with the same element touch, they fuse into one (and release energy)! This fusion will create the next largest atom that would naturally come next in the life cycle of a star. Keep going until you can merge two iron atoms.

Wait! Why don't the numbers add up?
When it comes to nuclear fusion inside a massive star, 2 + 2 does not equal 4. It's true the 1 + 1 = 2 (H + H = He), but after the Hydrogen in the core of a star is burned up, Helium begins to fuse into Carbon and Oxygen. Weird, right? Life inside a star's core is messy!
